GLOBALLOGIC OPERATIONAL POLICIES & GUIDELINES

1. VACATION AND LEAVE POLICY

GlobalLogic typically follows a localized leave policy based on the region of employment. 
- Earned Leave (EL/PL): Accrued monthly; usually allows for 18-24 days per year.
- Sick & Casual Leave (SL/CL): Approximately 12 days annually for personal exigencies or illness.
- Public Holidays: Standard gazetted holidays based on the office location (approx. 10-12 days).
- Maternity/Paternity Leave: Compliant with statutory laws (e.g., 26 weeks for maternity in India).
- Approval: All leaves must be applied for through the internal HRMS portal and approved by the reporting manager at least 2 weeks in advance for planned vacations.

2. WORK FROM HOME (WFH) & HYBRID GUIDELINES

GlobalLogic has transitioned to a "Flexible Hybrid" model.
- Schedule: Most roles require 2-3 days of office presence per week, though this varies by project and client requirements.
- Infrastructure: Employees are provided with a company laptop. A one-time or monthly internet reimbursement may be applicable depending on the specific contract/level.
- Core Hours: Employees are expected to be available during the team's core collaboration hours (typically 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM local time).
- Security: Use of company VPN is mandatory for all remote work to ensure data integrity.

3. EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT PROCESS

Expenses incurred for official purposes (travel, client meals, certifications) are reimbursed via the internal portal.
- Submission: Claims must be submitted through the 'Concur' or internal ERP tool.
- Documentation: Digital copies of valid tax invoices/receipts are mandatory for all claims.
- Timelines: Claims should ideally be submitted within 30 days of the expense.
- Approval: Primary approval by the Reporting Manager, followed by Finance Department verification.

4. CODE OF CONDUCT: DRESS CODE

GlobalLogic maintains a "Business Casual" environment.
- Monday to Thursday: Business casual (collared shirts, polos, chinos, dark jeans, blouses, or ethnic wear).
- Friday: Casual dress is permitted (T-shirts without offensive graphics, jeans, sneakers).
- Client Meetings: Professional business attire is expected when representing the company at client sites or during high-stakes virtual meetings.

5. ONBOARDING PROCESS

The "Day 1" experience is designed to integrate new hires seamlessly:
- Orientation: Introduction to GlobalLogic (a Hitachi Group Company) and the 5-pillar EVP.
- IT Provisioning: Issuance of hardware, security badges, and credentials.
- HR Induction: Overview of benefits, insurance enrollment, and payroll setup.
- Project Shadowing: New hires are typically assigned a 'Buddy' to help navigate the first 30 days.

6. BONUS AND INCENTIVE POLICY

Bonuses are performance-linked and distributed based on both individual and company achievements.
- Annual Performance Bonus: Typically paid out after the annual appraisal cycle (usually Q1 or Q2 depending on fiscal year alignment).
- Referral Bonus: Incentives for successful employee referrals once the candidate completes a 90-day probation.
- Project Excellence Awards: Spot bonuses or quarterly awards for exceptional delivery.
- Variable Pay: For senior roles, a percentage of the CTC is tied to specific KPIs and Hitachi Group's global performance.
